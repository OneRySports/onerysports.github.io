The top baseball movies of all time

Feb 09, 2026 • Mukwonago, WI
The Ry-Guy here - and I'm ready to analyze these top baseball films like they're games on the board! Baseball season is just around the corner, and what better way to get pumped than with a list of my favorite baseball movies? Let me dive into this and give you the rundown. A League of Their Own (1992) - This film follows Dottie Hinson (Geena Davis), star catcher for the Rockford Peaches in the Women's Baseball League during World War II. The movie showcases great performances from a talented cast, including Tom Hanks and Madonna. However, I'd be lying if I said this was my top pick. Major League (1989) - This one is an absolute classic! It tells the story of a group of misfit Cleveland Indians players who come together to save their team's chances of making it into the playoffs. The movie has plenty of laughs and heart, but let's be real here - it's not the most accurate representation of baseball in my opinion. Field of Dreams (1989) - Now we're talking! This film is more fantasy than realistic portrayal of the game. It tells the story of Ray Kinsella, a farmer who believes he hears voices telling him to build a baseball field in his cornfield so that long-deceased players could come back and play. While it may not be entirely accurate representation of how baseball works, this movie still manages to tug at our heartsstrings with its magical realism. Moneyball (2011) - This film is based on the book by Michael Lewis about Oakland A's general manager Billy Beane's revolutionary approach to building a winning team using statistical analysis instead of traditional scouting methods. Brad Pitt delivers an incredible performance as Beane, and Jonah Hill shines alongside him as his top assistant Peter Brand. This movie not only showcases great acting but also highlights how analytics have changed the way teams are built today in professional sports - which makes it my favorite film on this list!
