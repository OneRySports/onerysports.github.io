Red Sox get '25 ROY finalist Durbin from Crew in 6-player trade - mlb.com

Feb 10 • Mukwonago, WI
Here's my take on the Red Sox acquiring Durbin in that blockbuster trade with Milwaukee, my Brewers: Durbin joins a deep Red Sox system loaded with top prospects. He'll get a chance to prove himself at the big league level much sooner than he would have stuck around in Milwaukee. The Crew gave up some key pieces but still remain in contention this year and look set for success moving forward. This trade screams of Boston's win-now mentality under Chaim Bloom, sacrificing long-term depth for immediate impact. Durbin is a high-risk/high-reward bat that could be an X-factor on the right team - and the Red Sox just gave him his chance. It'll be interesting to see if he can live up to the hype in Fenway. As a Brewers fan, I'm disappointed but understand why they pulled the trigger. They're still stacked with talent from top to bottom after this deal. Durbin was going to have to fight for playing time behind Yelich and others, so moving him while they got value makes sense. He might just be better suited for a team like Boston where he can play more often right away. This is the kind of trade that could pay big dividends if Durbin hits it out of the park in Beantown. It's a calculated gamble by both sides - one that should keep both teams competitive down the stretch and into 2024. Here's hoping Durbin can make the most of this opportunity, as he was always someone I thought had the tools to be a difference maker at the highest level when given his shot.
