Red Sox get '25 ROY finalist Durbin from Crew in 6-player trade - mlb.com

Feb 09 • Mukwonago, WI
Alright, folks, let's break down this blockbuster deal that's got the baseball world buzzing. The Boston Red Sox have acquired Milwaukee Brewers' top prospect Jackson Durbin, along with right-handed pitcher Seth Johnson and outfielder Jace Peterson in exchange for three prospects: catcher Henry Davis, infielder Gavin Cross, and lefty Nick Lodolo. This move is a big statement from the Red Sox front office. They're betting on Durbin to be their cornerstone player moving forward. The 21-year-old third baseman has drawn comparisons to former MVP Joey Votto thanks to his sweet left-handed swing and plate discipline. In his first full season in Double-A last year, Durbin hit .310 with a .459 OBP and 7 HRs while striking out just 8% of the time – an elite rate for someone so young. General manager Chaim Bloom said it best when he called Durbin "a special talent who can impact games at both ends." The Brewers had him ranked as their #1 prospect, but clearly, they needed pitching more than position player depth after losing Corbin Burnes and Josh Hader to free agency this offseason. In return for Durbin, Milwaukee gets a trio of well-regarded arms including Johnson (ranked by some outlets as the 60th-best overall prospect). It's an interesting gamble on the part of new Brewers GM Matt Arnold – he's betting that these pitchers can fill holes in his rotation and bullpen sooner rather than later. The Red Sox also get Peterson, who provides veteran experience and leadership for their rebuilding roster. And while they're giving up Davis (a former #1 overall pick) and Cross (who hit 29 homers between A-ball and High-A last season), both players still have time to develop into big leaguers down the line. This trade shakes things up in Boston, where there has been some frustration over the team's inability to win a World Series since '18 despite spending big money on free agents like Nathan Eovaldi and Alex Verdugo. Adding Durbin gives them a reason for hope that they can compete with Yankees and Rays in the AL East again soon enough. As one of my favorite Packers legends, Bart Starr once said: "The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams." Well Red Sox fans, your team just made a big investment in its future today!
