Brewers trade Durbin, Monasterio to Boston for Harrison, Hamilton

Feb 09, 2026 • Mukwonago, WI
Well folks, buckle up because the Milwaukee Brewers just dropped a major bombshell on us! In what can only be described as a Monday-morning shocker in Milwaukee, the Brew Crew has traded away their third baseman Caleb Durbin, utility infielders Andruw Monasterio and Anthony Seigler, along with a competitive balance pick to the Boston Red Sox. The return for Milwaukee? Left-handed starter Kyle Harrison, versatile infielder David Hamilton, and minor league left-hander Shane Drohan. The move is head-scratcher on multiple levels. First off, trading Durbin – who was a big part of last year's 97-win team and earned 2.8 WAR in a promising rookie season – seems like an incredibly steep price to pay for Harrison. While the lefty did have some impressive minor league numbers with a 3.39 ERA over 350 innings, his major league career (42 appearances/194.2 IP) doesn't scream "game-changer" just yet, posting a 4.39 ERA and 4.43 FIP. Hamilton is certainly an interesting piece – he's a speedy defender with some versatility between second base and shortstop. But his bat hasn't quite developed as hoped, and the Brewers are giving up a significant amount of talent to acquire him. Drohan, while a talented minor leaguer who was excellent when healthy last year (47 2/3 IP at Triple-A Worcester: 2.27 ERA, 12.7 K/9), still needs more seasoning before he's ready for the big leagues. The biggest question now is – who is starting at third base on Opening Day? With Durbin gone, that position suddenly becomes wide open and could be one of the most intriguing positional battles this spring training. It also leaves a huge hole in the Brewers' lineup both offensively and defensively. The loss of Monasterio, while not as high-profile, is still significant given his solid performance and reliability over the past three seasons. Ry-Guy's Spread Analysis: This trade has some major implications for the Brewers from a betting perspective. Let's break it down: 1) Third Base Woes: The Brewers have now traded away their starting third baseman, leaving a significant hole in their lineup and on defense. This is not a value-add move – it’s a downgrade at a critical position. Expect the over/under for runs scored to drop accordingly. 2) Pitching Potential?: Harrison has some upside as a left-handed starter, but his major league performance so far hasn’t been stellar. Drohan could be interesting down the line if he develops into a solid option out of the bullpen or rotation, but he’s still in Triple-A and dealing with injury concerns. The return on this trade looks more like future potential than immediate value to me. 3) Utility Infielder Replacement: Hamilton is an adequate replacement for Monasterio at best – not a huge upgrade by any means. Losing Seigler, who showed some upside last year despite his struggles, further weakens the infield depth and flexibility. In summary, this trade feels like the Brewers are giving up too much for too little in return – especially considering they're trading away their starting third baseman. The potential upside is there with Harrison and Drohan down the line, but that doesn't help them now when it matters most: winning games. From a betting perspective, I'm downgrading my confidence on Milwaukee's chances this season until further developments are made at third base or in the rotation. Stay tuned folks – there's still plenty of time for more shakeups before Opening Day!
