NFL news roundup: Adam Gase joining Chargers' staff after years out of NFL; Eagles hiring OL coach - NFL.com

Feb 09 • Mukwonago, WI
Well folks, it's been quite the week in the world of NFL coaching hires and firings! I've got some updates from around the league that might interest you. First up, Adam Gase is making a comeback to the NFL after spending time out of the game since being fired by the Dolphins back in 2019. The Chargers have brought him on as an offensive assistant coach under head man Brandon Staley. Gase was once considered one of the league's brightest young coaching minds, having led both the Jets and Dolphins at a young age. It'll be interesting to see if he can translate that success into helping build up the Bolts' offense. In other news, the Eagles have filled out their offensive line coaching staff by hiring former Raiders O-line coach Tom Cable as an offensive assistant. Cable brings nearly 20 years of NFL experience with him and should be able to help out the Birds in developing their young talent on the interior lines. That's all for now, but stay tuned! As more moves are made around the league, I'll keep you updated here on The Ry-Guy Sports Network - your go-to source for all things football from Mukwonago, Wisconsin!
