Bucks vs. Magic Player Grades: Porter led, Rollins disappointed, and Thomas debuted in streak-ending loss

Feb 10 • Mukwonago, WI
## Porter's Heroics Can't Overcome Rollins' Meltdown, Thomas Debut as Bucks Suffer Streak-Ending Loss to Magic The Milwaukee Bucks put up a valiant effort against the Orlando Magic but ultimately fell short 118-99 in an ugly first half that saw both teams struggle from deep. However, it was the Magic’s dominant final 18 minutes that proved decisive, snapping a brief Bucks winning streak. With another matchup looming tomorrow night, there's still a chance for redemption—or perhaps a strategic tanking move for the pro-tank crowd—depending on how you look at it. **Player Grades Breakdown:** * **Myles Turner (Grade: B-):** While his stats were solid with 13 points and 6 rebounds in 27 minutes, missing key three-pointers was a major bummer. He did his job setting screens though, which is crucial for forcing switches on defense. Just couldn't drop those big threes when it mattered most – kind of like missing the final shot clock buzzer-beater that would've won it. * **Ryan Rollins (Grade: D):** Man, this was a rough one for Rollins. Starting slow and never finding his groove, he rushed tough shots, overpossessed leading to turnovers, and committed four fouls, playing too aggressively on defense. It looks like the Magic got into the bonus because of him – that's just not good. Definitely one of his worst games this season by a mile. * **AJ Green (Grade: B+):** Loved AJ's performance! He was aggressive, hitting 4/8 threes including that ridiculous side-step triple over Franz Wagner with time winding down – pure clutch! Did everything he needed to do in 32 minutes of work. * **Kevin Porter Jr. (Grade: A):** KPJ was the man for Milwaukee tonight. He dropped 28 points, dished out seven assists and grabbed four rebounds in 36 minutes despite making just one three-pointer. He hit a ton of crucial mid-range shots keeping the Bucks competitive, and his passing game was fantastic – finding open men consistently when he beat defenders off the dribble. That's the kind of playmaker we need more often! * **Bobby Portis (Grade: D):** Another tough night for Bobby against a team that absolutely shuts down his game. With just 10 points in 16 minutes, struggling to find scoring opportunities against Orlando’s size and athleticism was evident. He couldn't get into his backdown game or hit the pump fake – it's like facing an impenetrable wall out there! * **Kyle Kuzma (Grade: F):** Kuzma had one of those nights where everything went wrong - 3 points in 17 minutes with five turnovers. Not what you want from a guy who’s supposed to be a reliable scorer and playmaker. Basically, Portis was right but even worse! * **Gary Harris (Grade: C):** At least he got to catch up with his old Magic buddies, I guess? 0 points in 14 minutes – not exactly lighting it up, but also not committing major errors. It's a shame seeing him struggle this much after the Bucks invested in him; feels like they picked someone who just didn't quite fit the puzzle piece perfectly from day one. * **Cam Thomas (Grade: C):** Debuting and playing 13 minutes, Cam got some run but only managed four points on 1/5 shooting. Doc Rivers seems to be figuring out his role alongside Porter and Rollins – having two of them on the floor at all times could help spread things out more effectively. It’s early days for him though; gotta keep grinding! * **Jericho Sims (Grade: B):** Liked what I saw from Jericho tonight! He was active on both ends, setting great screens that opened up lob opportunities – something even Giannis would appreciate seeing! Plus, he stuck with guards on switches defensively. That’s solid two-way work in 27 minutes of playing time. * **Pete Nance (Grade: B):** Played sparingly but didn't do anything wrong from what I can recall - clean minutes is always good to see when you're coming back or adjusting your role. **Coaching & Strategy:** Doc Rivers did make some smart decisions regarding playing time – limiting Kuzma and Portis after they had negative impacts, while giving more minutes to Jericho Sims and Kevin Porter Jr., who both played well tonight. It was also a good call to bench Gary Trent Jr.; the Magic are one of those teams that needs guys who can really pierce their defense rather than just relying on stationary shooters – GTJ wasn't going to make much happen in this matchup. Overall, I thought Rivers coached fine game aside from maybe not having more answers for Orlando's aggression and physicality down the stretch. **Bonus Bucks Bits:** * Doc noted that physicality was a major factor in the Magic’s win; they came out harder on every possession. * KPJ pointed to aggressive doubling by the Magic as a big reason behind Milwaukee’s 19 turnovers – tough matchup for our ball handlers there! * Bobby Portis emphasized needing better boxing out from everyone, not just one guy hitting the glass. Even with zero rebounds himself tonight (which drew some online criticism), he's right about focusing on team rebounding efforts overall - it's all about effort and hustle! * There were funny quips online about people being shocked Cam Thomas didn’t shoot once during his first stint – fair enough! But he did get minutes, so progress is progress. * We got our first look at Ousmane Dieng in garbage time; I want to see him get more real playing opportunities soon too - maybe tomorrow night? * Franz Wagner returned after a long layoff and played well for Orlando – good to have another star back on the court! * Paolo Banchero… he's just not quite living up to that #1 overall pick hype right now, is he? His contract could be problematic down the line if this continues. * Anthony Black shows that sometimes development takes time and patience - you gotta build those skills piece by piece before everything clicks together! **Up Next:** The Bucks will face the Magic again tomorrow night at 6:00 p.m. CST on FanDuel Sports Network Wisconsin. Can they bounce back? We'll see if this was just a blip or another step in what some are calling a strategic tanking effort for future draft picks!
