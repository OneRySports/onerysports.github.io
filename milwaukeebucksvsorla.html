Milwaukee Bucks vs. Orlando Magic Preview & Game Thread: Now for a real test

Feb 10 • Mukwonago, WI
Alright, Bucks fans! We've got ourselves a game tonight against the Orlando Magic on Peacock at 6:30 p.m. CST. The Bucks have been on an impressive three-game win streak, but they need to prove that this isn't just beating up on bad teams. It's time for them to take on a playoff-caliber team like the Magic in order to show where they truly stand. Over the past few games against the Bulls, Pelicans, and Pacers, the Bucks have been putting up some solid numbers with an offensive rating that ranks second in the league (though we'll leave their defensive woes for another discussion). Ryan Rollins has been looking more like himself again after a 27-point performance in overtime against New Orleans. And Kevin Porter Jr., who's now back from his injury, has been putting up some impressive numbers as well. The Magic, on the other hand, have made an all-in trade for Desmond Bane but haven't quite lived up to their expectations this season. They're just barely above .500 and ranked 21st in offensive efficiency despite having Bane in the mix. However, they are a playoff team who can still give us a run if we don't bring our A-game. The Magic's defense has been their superpower in recent years but this season it sits at a mediocre 15th place. With that said, the Bucks will have to prove that they can shoot the ball well (and trust me, they've been doing just fine with their third-ranked EFG% so far). Anthony Black is going to be a key player for the Magic tonight. He's taken a giant leap this season and his stats across the board have skyrocketed. The Bucks will need to neutralize him if they want to come out on top in this matchup. So, as we sit down to watch the game tonight on Peacock, let's keep an eye on both teams' performances – especially since Giannis and Taurean Prince are sitting out for the Bucks while Franz Wagner is also sidelined for Orlando. This is a real test of where these teams stand in today’s NBA landscape and how they'll fare without some of their top players due to injury or other reasons. Let's cheer on our home team, Milwaukee Bucks! Go Bucks!
