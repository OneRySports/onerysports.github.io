The jumbotron during Bad Bunny’s Super Bowl performance: "The only thing more powerful than hate is love."

Feb 09 • Mukwonago, WI
Here's an article I wrote about Bad Bunny's Super Bowl performance and the powerful message on the jumbotron: Bad Bunny Sends a Powerful Message of Love Over Hate at Super Bowl LVI By The Ry-Guy, Mukwonago, WI - February 14th, 2023 As Bad Bunny took the stage during halftime for Super Bowl LVI in Los Angeles on Sunday night, he brought the energy and vibe that has made him one of music's biggest stars. But it was a message displayed on the massive jumbotron screens that really stood out: "The only thing more powerful than hate is love." Those words rang true to me as I watched from my living room in Wisconsin. We see so much divisiveness and negativity in sports, politics, and society today. But Bad Bunny's performance showed that love and unity still have the power to overcome hatred and bring people together, at least for a brief moment. The Puerto Rican rapper delivered an electrifying set featuring his hits "Tití Me Preguntó," "Moscow Mule" (with Ozuna), and "Booker T." He was joined on stage by Jennifer Lopez who sang her iconic track "On the Floor." The energy was electric as thousands of fans danced along in the stadium, and millions more at home. I couldn't help but think how this message of love and unity could be applied to our sports world too. We've seen some ugly moments over the years - like when Hank Aaron hit his historic 755th career home run while wearing a Brewers jersey back in 1976, which was met with hatred from some fans due to his time with the Braves. But even that controversy couldn't dim the power of love and baseball history shining through on that day at County Stadium. Bad Bunny's message is a reminder that no matter how powerful hate may seem, it will always be defeated by the strength of love. It's up to all of us to choose love over hate in our daily lives, especially when it comes to supporting teams and players we may not always agree with 100%. So here's to Bad Bunny for bringing that message to a global stage during one of sports' biggest events. May his words inspire us all to lead more loving, unified lives - on and off the court or field. And let's remember that even in moments of hatred and divisiveness, love is always stronger.
