The jumbotron during Bad Bunny’s Super Bowl performance: "The only thing more powerful than hate is love."

Feb 09 • Mukwonago, WI
Alright, listen up – we all know the NFL's a business first and foremost, but sometimes they drop a bomb that hits you right in the feels like a thunderous dunk. I'm talking about Bad Bunny's halftime show during Super Bowl LVII—no, not just his moves or beats—but what was plastered across the jumbotron: "The only thing more powerful than hate is love." Now, as a Wisconsin sports fan with a lifetime of refs’ whistles and game-winners under my belt? That line wasn't just some random lyric—it felt like a straight-up message to anyone who’d ever rooted against us Packer fans. I mean think about it: that stadium’s packed with every kind of fan, hating or loving their team, but Bad Bunny’s vibe was pure unity. It made me remember 2013 when Rodgers and Cobb pulled off that miracle TD on 4th & 8 at Soldier Field to clinch the division—*that* moment wasn’t about hate; it was about grit, love for the game, and standing together after every blown call or heartbreak. And speaking of history? Yeah, even before Monday Night Football became a weekly thing, the Pack had been on that stage back in 1964 during their simulated MNF experiment—proving we’ve always brought the drama. So while some might see it as just another flashy jumbotron message, I know it’s deeper than hype: it’s a reminder that when you’ve got love for your team and each other, all the haters in the world can’t stop what’s coming next. Whether you’re betting on spreads or defending a three-pointer at the buzzer (trust me, I’ve seen both), keep it real—love wins every time.
