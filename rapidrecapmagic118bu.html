Rapid Recap: Magic 118, Bucks 99

Feb 10 • Mukwonago, WI
Alright, let's dive into this one like it's a critical foul call at the buzzer—there are some serious issues here that need unpacking. I'm talking about tonight's Bucks game: **Magic 118, Bucks 99**. First off, the Bucks' win streak is over, and not just "over"—it ended in a flat-out blowout. Kevin Porter Jr., who was our only consistent offensive spark all night, dropped 28 points with seven assists and four rebounds. But even KPJ couldn't carry this team when it mattered most. The game started rough for both sides—a combined 10 turnovers (seven by the Bucks) and 21 missed shots in the first quarter alone? That's a recipe for disaster, not success on the court. Both teams were about level for the first six minutes or so until Franz Wagner came back after recovering from an ankle injury—talk about bad timing! He quickly scored twice in transition and upped Orlando’s pace, making it easier for them to create offense. Then there's Cam Thomas entering the game wearing those flashy Kobe Grinch shoes (which I thought were pretty cool). The Bucks had tied things up at 31 within three minutes following multiple buckets from Bobby Portis before Anthony Black went wild with triples and nearly detonated at the rim—putting the Magic ahead by eight points. Thankfully, Rollins and Green hit crucial threes to keep us within shouting distance even as Orlando was throwing brick after brick from deep (they were 3/20!). The first half of third quarter was nip-and-tuck with Porter, Turner, and Green carrying our scoring load—but we still only managed to go down two points before Orlando made their move. That included a smooth fake-euro-step-I-don't-know-what from Anthony Black (watch the replay—it's nuts!). Then they finally got some threes going which blew this game wide open—Moe and Franz Wagner each had one as did Magic first-round draftee Jase Richardson; after shooting 4/21 in the first half, they went 6/11 in third quarter alone! We saw Ousmane Dieng knock in a smooth bucket during garbage time on the plus side. But here's where it hurts: **the Bucks got beaten at possession game**. We shot 77 shots to their 86—this was due largely to our turnover problems (we had 19, they only had 11). Also, Orlando had 11 offensive rebounds compared to Milwaukee’s six. So what does this all mean? It means that tonight wasn't just about losing—it was about losing in every single facet of the game. We turned over the ball too much and gave up easy points on offense and defense alike. There's no sugarcoating it: we need to step back onto that court next week with our heads held high but our game plan dialed in tighter than ever before—because if we don't, then I'm afraid another loss like tonight could be brewing around the corner.
